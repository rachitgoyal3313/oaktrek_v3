Frequently Asked Questions

Curious about our sustainability efforts? Here are answers to some common questions.

How do you measure your carbon footprint? +

We work with third-party environmental experts to calculate the carbon footprint of each product across its entire lifecycle—from materials and manufacturing to transportation, use, and end-of-life. This comprehensive approach, known as a Life Cycle Assessment (LCA), helps us identify where we can make the biggest improvements.

What makes your materials sustainable? +

We evaluate materials based on multiple sustainability criteria: renewable resource use, water efficiency, carbon impact, chemical use, and end-of-life options. We prioritize natural, renewable materials that can be grown with minimal environmental impact and processed using responsible methods.

How do you ensure ethical manufacturing? +

All our manufacturing partners must adhere to our strict Code of Conduct, which covers fair labor practices, worker safety, and environmental standards. We regularly audit our factories, both announced and unannounced, and work closely with factory management to implement continuous improvements.

What is regenerative agriculture? +

Regenerative agriculture refers to farming practices that restore soil health, increase biodiversity, improve watershed health, and sequester carbon from the atmosphere. These practices include rotational grazing, cover cropping, composting, and minimizing soil disturbance. We're working with our wool suppliers to transition to these methods.

How can I recycle my old OakTrek shoes? +

Through our ReWalk program, you can bring your old OakTrek shoes to any of our retail locations or request a prepaid shipping label to send them back to us. Shoes in good condition are donated to those in need, while worn-out pairs are disassembled and the materials are recycled into new products.
